Microneedling

Microneedling is a minimally invasive cosmetic procedure that uses tiny, sterile needles to create micro-injuries in the skin. These controlled micro-injuries stimulate the body’s natural collagen and elastin production, promoting smoother, firmer, and more youthful-looking skin. Also known as collagen induction therapy, microneedling can help reduce the appearance of scars, fine lines, and uneven skin texture.

During microneedling, our professionals use specialized devices to carefully treat targeted areas of the skin. Most patients experience minimal discomfort with little to no downtime. In some cases, topical skincare products may be used alongside the procedure to enhance results.

Comprehensive Skin Rejuvenation Benefits

By boosting collagen and elastin, microneedling helps your skin heal and renew itself. This treatment can be personalized to address individual concerns, resulting in a more radiant, even-toned, and rejuvenated complexion.

  • Improved Skin Texture
  • Reduced Acne Scars
  • Minimized Fine Lines and Wrinkles
  • Enhanced Collagen Production
  • Increased Skin Firmness
  • Refined Pore Size
  • Brighter, More Youthful Appearance
  • Enhanced Absorption of Skincare Products

Got Questions about Microneedling?

Our experts are here to help you understand the microneedling process and how it can transform your skin. Below are some common questions we receive.

Most patients describe microneedling as mildly uncomfortable rather than painful. A topical numbing cream can be applied to help ensure your comfort during the procedure.

The number of sessions varies based on your skin concerns and desired outcomes. Many individuals see noticeable improvements after 3–6 treatments spaced a few weeks apart.

Mild redness and slight swelling are common immediately following microneedling. This typically subsides within a day or two. Proper aftercare and sun protection are advised to optimize healing.

Microneedling is considered safe for most skin types. However, it’s important to consult with a professional to determine if it’s the right treatment for you, especially if you have active acne, rosacea, or other specific concerns.

Common side effects include temporary redness, mild swelling, or sensitivity. Severe complications are rare when performed by trained professionals following proper sterilization and treatment protocols.
